What is Cytoplasm?



The Cytoplasm project is a revolutionary new approach to medical technology. It intends to bring much-needed modernisation to an increasingly antiquated healthcare system.


In the past, even for a routine checkup, patients with cardiovascular conditions were required to stay in hospital overnight, connected to bulky, ageing machines. But, with co-operation from ARM and the UCL Electronic Engineering department, we are creating a series of discrete, lightweight wearable sensors that will transmit data wirelessly to a centralised network. This network can be accessed by doctors from almost any device: such as a tablet, web browser or even a mobile phone - bringing unprecedented simplicity and innovation to treating cardiovascular conditions.

Who is Cytoplasm?



The Cytoplasm team consists of: Ellery Smith (hardware), Sam Payne (cloud services), Ernest Chen and Caitlin Jiang (end-user interface), with assistance from the UCL Electronic Engineering department.
